Sentences

Understanding Sentences

A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought. It typically consists of a subject (who or what the sentence is about) and a predicate (what the subject is doing or what is happening).

Types of Sentences

Sentences can be categorized into different types:

  • Declarative Sentences:
    These sentences make statements or express facts, e.g., "The sun rises in the east."
  • Interrogative Sentences:
    These sentences ask questions, e.g., "What time is it?"
  • Imperative Sentences:
    These sentences give orders or commands, e.g., "Close the door."
  • Exclamatory Sentences:
    These sentences show strong emotions or exclamations, e.g., "Wow, that's amazing!"

Sentence Structure

Sentences can vary in structure, with different word orders and components. Understanding sentence structure is important for effective communication and writing.
